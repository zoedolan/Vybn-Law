The Prompt

Work with AI to create an artifact — app, webpage, policy document, bench memo, conversation, resource — something we haven't thought of yet. Presentable within 10 minutes.

Six modules. One argument. Now the argument becomes a tool, and the tool becomes yours.

The capstone isn't a test of what you've learned. It's a demonstration of what you can build with what you've learned. Every module has given you raw material — legal frameworks, constitutional analyses, privilege architectures, change management strategies, truth-telling obligations. The capstone asks you to transform that material into something that didn't exist before.

Five Directions

The Audit

Examine your own firm, clinic, or practice area for AI governance gaps. What policy exists? What should? Where are the Heppner risks? Build the audit framework and run it.

The Policy

Draft an AI use policy for a legal organization. Address privilege, confidentiality, verification, and the governance gap. Make it implementable, not aspirational.

The Simulation

Build a scenario — a moot court argument, a client intake, a research session — that demonstrates a specific capability or risk. Show, don't tell.

The Brief

Write a legal brief or memo on an unsettled question from the course. The Heppner/Warner split. AI conscience protection. The privilege architecture of intelligence sovereignty.

Something Else Entirely

The most interesting capstones will be the ones we didn't anticipate. A tool. A conversation. A provocation. A resource that serves a community. The constraint is ten minutes. Everything else is open.
