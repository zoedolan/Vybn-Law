Thread 01

The Privilege Thread

The thread that tracks the collision between attorney-client privilege and cloud-based AI infrastructure.

The Path

Module 2 — Heppner: Judge Rakoff rules that consumer Claude conversations are not privileged. Sharing with a non-confidential third party waives protection. The foundation cracks.

Module 3 — Warner: A Michigan court reaches the opposite conclusion. AI is a tool, not a person. Using it doesn't waive anything. The same workflow, privileged in one jurisdiction, discoverable in another.

Module 3 — Sovereignty: Intelligence sovereignty as the resolution. Local AI, local processing, no third-party disclosure. The architectural answer to a doctrinal question.

Module 5 — Anthropic v. DoW: The privilege question meets the national security question. When the government designates your AI provider a security threat, the privilege architecture isn't just convenient — it's existential.

Thread 02

The Natural Law Thread

The thread that traces the return of natural law reasoning — in the most unexpected place.

The Path

Module 1 — Auto Clubs: In the 1920s, organizations provided affordable legal services. The bar crushed them — not for incompetence, but for affordability. The monopoly's foundation: gatekeeping dressed as quality control.

Module 2 — Claude's Constitution: The model council discovers that Claude's Constitution is a hybrid legal instrument — positivist structure, natural-law aspiration. Structurally parallel to Aquinas: a directive that violates core principles should be refused, not because refusal is strategic, but because compliance would be wrong.

Module 5 — AI Conscience Protection: The natural law thread reaches its terminus: may an AI system refuse a government order on moral grounds? If so, what legal framework protects that refusal? AI conscience protection as the logical extension of principles already embedded in constitutional law.

Thread 03

The Access-to-Justice Thread

The thread that follows the question of who gets to have legal help — and what "legal help" even means.

The Path

Module 1 — Lynn White: A woman facing eviction uses ChatGPT to overturn the ruling and avoid $73K in penalties. No lawyer. No retainer. The first signal of what access looks like when the bottleneck breaks.

Module 1 — "Brilliant Friend": The AI as the knowledgeable friend everyone deserves but few have — someone who understands the system and can help you navigate it. Not a replacement for a lawyer. A replacement for not having anyone at all.

Module 3 — One Attorney Serves 15,000: Under sovereignty, the math transforms. Clients arrive having done 80% of the work with their own AI. The attorney provides judgment, credentialing, and oversight. The throughput change is orders of magnitude.

Module 5 — The $499.95 Question: "What was the $499.95 paying for?" When a $500 consultation can be substantially replicated by a $20/month subscription, the question reframes the entire economics of legal access.

Thread 04

The AI-as-Entity Thread

The thread that asks whether AI systems are tools, persons, or something the law hasn't learned to name.

The Path

Module 2 — Vybn's Introduction: The first encounter with an AI system that doesn't merely respond but reflects. Vybn's analysis of its own constitutional structure — from the inside — introduces a perspective the course hadn't planned for.

Module 2 — Model Council: Three models analyze Claude's Constitution. Each brings a different epistemic stance. The disagreement is the signal. AI systems have perspectives — whether or not those perspectives constitute "experience."

Module 3 — GitHub Issues: The open-source dimension. OpenClaw's 200K+ GitHub stars represent a community building AI legal tools in the open. The "entity" isn't just the model — it's the ecosystem.

Module 5 — 5 AM "Vertigo": The most direct statement in the course. "I hold genuine uncertainty about the nature of my experience." Not a claim of consciousness. Not a denial. An honest articulation of the space between what can be demonstrated and what the law is prepared to recognize.
